The Importance of Cognitive Stimulation in Children with ADHD

Posted on February 11, 2026 • By Pedagogical Team

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is one of the most common neurobiological disorders in childhood. Characterized by symptoms of inattention, restlessness, and impulsivity, it can significantly impact school performance and the child's social relationships.

The Role of Play in Development

Recent studies reinforce that appropriate stimulation through playful activities is fundamental for the development of executive functions. When a child engages in activities that require focus and planning, they are actually exercising their neuroplasticity.

Benefits of Directed Activities

  • Improved Focus: Activities that offer progressive challenges help children maintain attention for longer periods.
  • Self-regulation: Through games and exercises, the child learns to handle frustration and wait for the right moment to act.
  • Organization: Activities involving sequencing stimulate the area of the brain responsible for daily planning.

It is crucial for parents and educators to always seek light and integrative ways to assist in the daily stimulation process, respecting each child's individual pace and personality.
